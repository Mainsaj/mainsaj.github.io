About Me

Qualifications:

  • Senior Moves in the Field
  • Novice Freeskate
  • PSA Member

My name is Alex Mains and I have spent most of my life in the figure skating community.

Before I can even remember my dad wanted me to follow in his foot steps and become a hockey player. He put me into beginner hockey classes but was frustrated because I could not skate. My mom suggested that I learn how to skate and learn hockey after and they enrolled me in ice skating classes.

Ice skating was my fun winter activity and I slowly moved up the levels until I was about ten. It was then that I told my mom I wanted to skate all year round. I began taking private lessons and the rest is history.

I began skating competitively soon after and in 2012 I placed 4th at the Upper Great Lakes Regional Championships at the Intermediate level and qualified for the Junior National Championships where I placed 21st.

When I graduated high school I moved to Colorado Springs to begin training with the Olympic Training Center at the Colorado Springs World Arena. I trained there for just over a year before I decided to end my competitive career to attend college.

However after a short time I decided that I missed the sport and wanted to reinvolve myself in some way. It was then that I decided to become a coach that I might pass my knowledge onto the next generation of skaters.
